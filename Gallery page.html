Stage 1: Basic Modifications

This stage focuses on simple, bolt-on modifications that improve the car's performance or aesthetics without significant engine work. Theyâ€™re usually designed for everyday drivers who want a little more power or style.

  • Performance: Cold air intake, cat-back exhaust, performance chip/ECU tune.
  • Appearance: New wheels, tires, spoilers, or cosmetic upgrades like new lights.
  • Suspension: Basic lowering springs or performance shocks.

Stage 2: Intermediate Modifications

At this stage, you start to make more significant changes to the car that can have a noticeable impact on performance.

  • Performance: Upgraded turbocharger/supercharger, bigger intercooler, high-flow fuel injectors, or a larger exhaust system.
  • Suspension: Coilovers for adjustable ride height, sway bars, and strut braces.
  • Brakes: Performance brake pads, slotted rotors.
  • ECU Tuning: More advanced ECU remapping for better power delivery.

  • Performance: Larger turbo or supercharger systems, full exhaust system replacement (headers, downpipes, etc.), upgraded internal engine components (pistons, rods, cams).
  • Suspension: Race-level coilovers, fully adjustable suspension setups.
  • Brakes: Big brake kits, carbon-ceramic rotors.
  • Drivetrain: Strengthened gearbox, upgraded clutch, limited-slip differential.
  • Tuning: Full standalone ECU, dyno tuning for maximum power and efficiency.

Stage 3: Advanced Modifications

This stage is reserved for cars that will be used for track racing or competitive motorsports. At this point, the car is heavily modified to achieve the best performance possible.

  • Engine: Full forged internals, upgraded turbo/supercharger to extreme levels, nitrous injection, or even an engine swap for more power.
  • Suspension & Handling: Full race suspension, custom-built roll cage, stripped interior to reduce weight.
  • Weight Reduction: Lightweight body panels, racing seats, and removal of unnecessary interior components.
  • Fuel System: Upgraded fuel pumps, high-flow injectors, methanol or race fuel systems.
